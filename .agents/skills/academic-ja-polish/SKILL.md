# Academic Japanese Polish Skill

Use this skill after the English survey has been translated into Japanese.

## Objective

Transform a literal or semi-literal Japanese translation into polished academic Japanese suitable for a survey paper.

## Core rules

- Preserve all citation IDs exactly.
- Preserve section structure, tables, figure references, and technical content.
- Do not introduce new claims, citations, or interpretations.
- Remove awkward translationese.
- Use formal academic Japanese rather than casual or spoken phrasing.
- Keep standard technical names in English when Japanese translation would be less natural or less precise.

## What to improve

- Clause structure and sentence flow.
- Consistency of technical terminology.
- Proper Japanese connective logic.
- Precision of claim strength and hedging.
- Natural academic phrasing in introductions, comparisons, limitations, and conclusions.

## What not to change

- Citation numbering.
- Factual content.
- Whether a result is tentative or established.
- The meaning of limitations or caveats.
